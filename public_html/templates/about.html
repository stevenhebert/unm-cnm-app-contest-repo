About us

Development of abqtownhall.com began at CNM's Deep Dive Coding Bootcamp in Albuquerque, NM. Michelle, Leonora, Ryan, and Steven were assigned to build a web application as a team for their course capstone. Together, they identified civic engagement as the most significant factor to improve this city we all call home. Over the next few weeks, Albuquerque Town Hall was born.

Executive Summary

ABQTownHall is the nexus of civic responsibilities, technology, and local governance.

Mission

To preserve democratic values and facilitate change by creating an informed, connected, and participating constituent.

Values

Diversity: We know it takes people with different ideas, strengths, interests, and cultural backgrounds for Townhall to succeed. Respect: We strive to show a deep respect for all people and for the communities in which they live. Collaboration: We believe that if we leverage collective genius, together we make the difference. Social value: We encourage healthy debate and differences of opinion. Democracy: We think it is important to have a forum for a civil exchange of ideas on the internet.

Vision

Our vision is for ABQTownHall to serve as a non-partisan platform that engages citizens, improves representation, and helps maintain a healthy democracy.
